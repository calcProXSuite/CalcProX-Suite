What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight. The formula is: BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]². It helps determine whether you're underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

Why is BMI Important?

BMI is used by doctors and health professionals as a general indicator of health. It can be an early sign of potential health problems like diabetes, heart disease, or malnutrition.

Limitations of BMI

BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat. A muscular person may have a high BMI but still be healthy. Similarly, BMI may not be accurate for older adults, children, or pregnant women.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is BMI accurate?

A: It's a good general guideline but not a perfect measure of health.

Q: What BMI is considered obese?

A: A BMI of 30 or above is classified as obese.

Q: Can BMI change daily?

A: Minor changes can occur based on water intake and food, but long-term trends matter most.
